### **1. From Determination to Belonging – My First Step into Computer Engineering**

**a) The Dream and the Struggle to Enter UET**
Getting into Computer Engineering was not easy for me. I attempted the entry test twice for UET Lahore, hoping to secure admission in my desired campus, but I couldn’t make it there. However, when my name appeared in the first merit list for the Faisalabad campus, it felt like a major achievement. Despite not being my first choice, it was still a doorway to my dream field. My journey reflects persistence, as I did not give up even after facing initial setbacks.

**b) First Impression and Unexpected Challenges**
On 8th September 2025, we traveled from Sargodha to Faisalabad full of excitement, but unfortunately, the university was closed due to extended holidays. We had to return, and that moment was difficult for me, especially when I faced disappointment from my father for not confirming earlier. When I finally saw the campus, I was shocked because it was very different from the Lahore campus I had imagined—quieter, less developed, and unfamiliar. I felt anxious and uncertain about how I would adjust to this new environment.

**c) Choosing Passion Over Pressure & Beginning with PF**
Despite all doubts, I stayed committed to my goal. Many people around me believed I should become a doctor, but I always knew that engineering—especially Computer Engineering—was my true passion. I wanted to enter the world of programming because I believe the future belongs to coders and innovators. On 15th September 2025, I returned to the university, and it marked my first official day. ### **2. Hostel Life – From Nervous Beginnings to Personal Growth**

**a) Adjusting to a New Environment**
My second journey began with hostel life, which was completely different from my previous experiences. Although I had stayed in a hostel before during my entry test preparation, it was a comfortable and peaceful environment with a single room. However, arriving late at the university hostel meant I was assigned a shared hall where eight students had to live together. I was only the second person there at that time, and the rest were yet to arrive. Being a naturally shy person, I felt extremely nervous, especially because some senior students were already present in the room. Fortunately, I met one girl who later turned out to be from Computer Engineering as well, which gave me a sense of familiarity and comfort.

**b) Challenges, Seniors, and Hostel Realities**
In the beginning, hostel life was not easy. After a few days, the seniors shifted to another room, and gradually the space started to feel like my own. However, new challenges appeared. The hostel had only one kitchen, and seniors often did not allow juniors to use it freely, which created difficulties. At times, there were conflicts between seniors and juniors, even leading to ragging sessions where juniors were called and questioned. These moments were stressful—one of my friends even became emotional and started crying, although I managed to stay calm. Over time, our room filled up with seven girls, all from different cities, each with a unique personality. Living together required patience, understanding, and adjustment.

**c) Learning Independence and Academic Support (PF & Beyond)**
Despite the challenges, hostel life became a source of growth and learning for me. For the first time, I experienced traveling alone, which built my confidence and taught me how to handle different types of people. Living away from home made me miss my family and especially homemade food, but it also prepared me for future independence. There were also many positive aspects—like not having to wake up too early for the bus and having a supportive academic environment. Students from Computer Science lived in the room next to ours, and they often helped us understand Programming Fundamentals (PF), especially topics like Python basics, functions, loops, and modules. During PF projects, we collaborated as a group, which improved our understanding. In return, I helped others with Calculus, creating a supportive learning circle that made both academic and hostel life easier and more meaningful.
