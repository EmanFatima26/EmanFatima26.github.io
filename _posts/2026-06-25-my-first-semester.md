Stepping into my university life, the first semester brought a massive transition for me. I had to manage a heavy workload of 9 different subjects. Coming from a pre-medical (Biology) background, the biggest and most intimidating challenge for me was **Programming Fundamentals (PF)**. Transitioning from biological sciences to writing logical code felt like entering a completely different world, and initially, it felt quite tough. However, we were fortunate to have **Dr. Bilal** as our instructor, whose incredible teaching style made this challenging journey remarkably smooth and understandable.

### The Foundation: First Weeks of Python

Right from the first week, Dr. Bilal focused on breaking down the basics, starting with Python syntax. What made it even better was that he conducted both our theory classes and practical lab sessions. In our very first lab, he taught us essential developer workflows, like creating a virtual environment and executing basic Python code. By the second week, we moved on to implementing simple arithmetic programs, such as addition and subtraction, which helped me build hands-on familiarity with the computer screen and compiler.

### Challenges in the Lab & Overcoming Them

As we entered the third week, we dived deeper into conditional logic, studying `if-else` structures in detail and writing programs to calculate grades based on student percentages. However, that week’s lab brought a real-world technical hurdle for me: my internet connection completely failed. Dr. Bilal had shared the lab files on a local server, and since I was new to all this, I had no idea how to fetch files from a server. Feeling stuck, I reached out to a classmate who helped me by sharing the file directly. Though I managed to complete the lab tasks, Sir Bilal conducted an evaluation that day. Since I hadn't fully grasped the theoretical depth of that topic yet, I could only answer a few questions. This taught me a valuable lesson about balancing theory with practical work.

### Moving Towards the Mid-Exams

Before the mid-term exams, our curriculum progressed into more complex programming concepts like loops, functions, and modular programming—creating modules with different functions and calling them inside a `main()` function. Initially, this conceptual layer felt overwhelming and confusing. Sensing our struggle, Sir Bilal stepped in during the lab sessions and gave us proper, step-by-step demonstrations until the concepts clicked. Soon after, our Mid-Exams began, and Sir’s PF theory paper was a true test of our understanding, filled with conceptual questions that demanded deep logic rather than rote learning.

### Ditching the Basics for Machine Learning

The post-mid-term phase shifted our learning into overdrive. Beyond the remaining Python concepts, we jumped straight into the world of Artificial Intelligence with a **Machine Learning-based project**. Dr. Bilal took the time to properly explain advanced concepts like Classification and Regression. For our project, we sourced an international dataset from a store in India and trained a predictive model designed to identify high-sales products and differentiate between high-priced and low-priced items. 

### The Ultimate Presentation Night Drama

The day of the project presentation brought immense pressure because it was the very first presentation of our university life. The night before was pure chaos—I stayed awake until 4:00 AM perfecting our project report. To make matters worse, the printer refused to work in the morning! Desperate, we had to coordinate with friends at the boys' hostel to finally get our reports printed just in time. 

When the presentations began, the nervous energy in the room was palpable. By the time my group partner and I were called to the stage, my blood pressure was literally dropping due to sheer anxiety and stress. Despite the nerves, we pushed through and delivered a good presentation. Looking back, while our technical execution was successful, our actual depth of learning at that stage wasn't as profound as it should have been—but it was an incredible stepping stone.

### A Semester to Remember

Just like that, amidst the endless loops, debugging errors, printing mishaps, and sleepless nights, my first semester flew by. Going from a student who didn't know how to create a virtual environment to training a Machine Learning model was a massive leap. I am incredibly grateful to Dr. Bilal for his patience and guidance, which turned a tough pre-med transition into an unforgettable foundational semester of computing.
